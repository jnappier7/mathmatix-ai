# Comprehensive Vertical Alignment Skill Map

This document traces the progression of mathematical logic from basic numeracy through multivariable calculus.

---

## Phase 1: Foundations (K - Grade 5)

### Kindergarten: Numeracy & Shapes

**Counting & Cardinality:**
- Count to 100 by ones and tens; count forward from a given number.

**Number Names:**
- Write numbers 0–20; represent a number of objects with a written numeral.

**One-to-One Correspondence:**
- Understand that the last number name said tells the number of objects counted.

**Comparison:**
- Compare two numbers between 1 and 10 presented as written numerals.

**Addition/Subtraction Concepts:**
- Understand addition as putting together and subtraction as taking apart.

**Decomposing Numbers:**
- Decompose numbers less than or equal to 10 into pairs (e.g., $5 = 2 + 3$).

**Making 10:**
- Find the number that makes 10 when added to any number 1–9.

**Measurement Attributes:**
- Describe measurable attributes of objects (length, weight).

**Classifying Objects:**
- Classify objects into given categories; count the numbers of objects in each category.

**2D Shapes:**
- Identify and describe squares, circles, triangles, rectangles, and hexagons.

**3D Shapes:**
- Identify cubes, cones, cylinders, and spheres.

**Positional Words:**
- Describe relative positions (above, below, beside, in front of).

---

### Grades 1-2: Fluency & Place Value

**Addition/Subtraction Fluency:**
- Add and subtract within 20 using mental strategies.

**Place Value (Tens/Ones):**
- Understand that the two digits of a two-digit number represent amounts of tens and ones.

**Comparison:**
- Compare two-digit numbers based on meanings of the tens and ones digits.

**Properties of Operations:**
- Apply commutative and associative properties.

**Missing Numbers:**
- Determine the unknown whole number in an addition or subtraction equation ($8 + ? = 11$).

**Measurement (Length):**
- Measure the length of an object by iterating length units.

**Time:**
- Tell and write time in hours and half-hours using analog and digital clocks.

**Data Representation:**
- Organize, represent, and interpret data with up to three categories.

**Money:**
- Solve word problems involving dollar bills, quarters, dimes, nickels, and pennies.

**Geometry Attributes:**
- Distinguish between defining attributes (e.g., triangles are closed and three-sided) vs. non-defining attributes (color).

**Partitioning:**
- Partition circles and rectangles into two, three, or four equal shares (intro to fractions).

**Even/Odd:**
- Determine whether a group of objects (up to 20) has an odd or even number of members.

---

### Grades 3-5: Operations & Fractions

**Multiplication/Division Facts:**
- Fluency with products of two one-digit numbers.

**Properties of Multiplication:**
- Distributive, associative, and commutative properties.

**Multi-Step Word Problems:**
- Solve problems using the four operations.

**Place Value (Multi-digit):**
- Rounding whole numbers; reading/writing numbers to 1,000,000.

**Fraction Equivalence:**
- Explain why a fraction $a/b$ is equivalent to a fraction $(n \times a)/(n \times b)$.

**Fraction Arithmetic:**
- Add and subtract fractions with like and unlike denominators.

**Multiplying Fractions:**
- Multiply a fraction by a whole number or another fraction.

**Decimals:**
- Understand decimal notation for fractions (tenths/hundredths) and compare decimals.

**Area & Perimeter:**
- Apply formulas for rectangles and complex rectilinear figures.

**Volume Concepts:**
- Measure volume by counting unit cubes; apply $V = l \times w \times h$.

**Coordinate Plane (Intro):**
- Graph points in the first quadrant.

**Line Plots:**
- Represent measurement data involving fractions.

**Factors & Multiples:**
- Find all factor pairs for a whole number in the range 1–100.

---

## Phase 2: Middle School (Grades 6-8)

### The Bridge to Algebra

**Ratios & Rates:**
- Concept of a ratio; unit rate; using ratio tables.

**Percentages:**
- Find a percent of a quantity as a rate per 100; solve problems involving finding the whole.

**Rational Number System:**
- Understand positive and negative integers; absolute value; ordering rational numbers.

**Arithmetic with Rationals:**
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers and fractions (including negatives).

**Algebraic Expressions:**
- Write, read, and evaluate expressions in which letters stand for numbers.

**One-Variable Equations:**
- Solve equations of the form $x + p = q$ and $px = q$; extend to multi-step equations.

**Inequalities:**
- Write and solve inequalities; graph solutions on a number line.

**Proportional Relationships:**
- Identify the constant of proportionality (unit rate) in tables, graphs, and equations ($y = kx$).

**Geometry Formulas:**
- Area of triangles/polygons; Surface area and volume of prisms/pyramids.

**Statistics:**
- Mean, median, mode, range; Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD); box plots.

**Probability:**
- Simple events; theoretical vs. experimental probability; compound events.

**Exponents (Intro):**
- Properties of integer exponents; scientific notation.

**Linear Functions:**
- Graphing linear equations ($y = mx + b$); understanding slope as rate of change.

**Systems of Equations (Intro):**
- Solve systems of two linear equations graphically and algebraically.

**Pythagorean Theorem:**
- Apply to determine unknown side lengths in right triangles and distance between points.

---

## Phase 3: High School Core

### Algebra 1

**Solving Linear Equations:**
- Multi-step equations; variables on both sides; literal equations.

**Linear Functions:**
- Domain and range; function notation $f(x)$; intercepts; slope-intercept and point-slope forms.

**Linear Inequalities:**
- Solving and graphing inequalities in one and two variables.

**Systems of Equations/Inequalities:**
- Substitution, elimination, and graphing methods.

**Exponents & Radicals:**
- Laws of exponents; simplifying radicals; rational exponents.

**Polynomials:**
- Adding, subtracting, and multiplying polynomials.

**Factoring:**
- GCF; difference of squares; trinomials ($a=1$ and $a \neq 1$).

**Quadratic Equations:**
- Solving by factoring, taking square roots, and the Quadratic Formula.

**Quadratic Functions:**
- Graphing parabolas; vertex form vs. standard form; axis of symmetry; max/min.

**Exponential Functions:**
- Growth and decay models; comparing linear vs. exponential growth.

**Data Analysis:**
- Scatter plots; lines of best fit; correlation vs. causation.

**Sequences:**
- Arithmetic and geometric sequences (recursive and explicit formulas).

---

### Geometry

**Foundations:**
- Points, lines, planes, segments, and angles.

**Logic & Proof:**
- Conditional statements; inductive/deductive reasoning; two-column proofs.

**Parallel & Perpendicular Lines:**
- Transversals; angle pair relationships; slopes of parallel/perpendicular lines.

**Congruent Triangles:**
- SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL congruence theorems.

**Relationships within Triangles:**
- Bisectors, medians, altitudes; Triangle Inequality Theorem.

**Quadrilaterals:**
- Properties of parallelograms, rectangles, rhombi, squares, trapezoids, and kites.

**Similarity:**
- Dilations; AA, SAS, SSS similarity theorems; proportionality in triangles.

**Right Triangles & Trigonometry:**
- Pythagorean Theorem; Special Right Triangles (45-45-90, 30-60-90); SohCahToa.

**Circles:**
- Tangents, chords, and secants; arc length and sector area; equation of a circle.

**3D Figures:**
- Surface area and volume of prisms, cylinders, pyramids, cones, and spheres.

**Transformations:**
- Translations, reflections, rotations, and dilations on the coordinate plane.

**Geometric Probability:**
- Probability based on length or area.

---

### Algebra 2 / Trigonometry

**Absolute Value Functions:**
- Graphing and solving absolute value equations/inequalities.

**Systems of 3 Variables:**
- Solving $3 \times 3$ systems.

**Matrix Operations:**
- Addition, multiplication, determinants, and inverses (optional in some curricula).

**Complex Numbers:**
- Operations with $i$; complex conjugates; roots of quadratics.

**Polynomial Functions:**
- Long and synthetic division; Remainder and Factor Theorems; End behavior; Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.

**Rational Functions:**
- Simplifying, multiplying/dividing expressions; solving rational equations; vertical/horizontal asymptotes.

**Radical Functions:**
- Solving radical equations; graphing square/cube root functions; inverse functions.

**Exponential & Logarithmic Functions:**
- Properties of logs; solving exponential/log equations; natural base $e$.

**Conic Sections (Intro):**
- Parabolas, circles, ellipses, hyperbolas.

**Trigonometric Ratios:**
- The Unit Circle; radians vs. degrees; reference angles.

**Trig Graphs:**
- Graphing sine, cosine, tangent with transformations (amplitude, period, phase shift).

**Trig Identities:**
- Reciprocal, quotient, and Pythagorean identities.

**Probability Distributions:**
- Normal distribution; z-scores; standard deviation.

---

## Phase 4: Advanced Mathematics

### Pre-Calculus

**Composite & Inverse Functions:**
- Verifying inverses; restricted domains.

**Polynomial & Rational Analysis:**
- Analyzing roots, turning points, and concavity (intro).

**Exponential & Log Modeling:**
- Logistic growth models; Newton's Law of Cooling.

**Analytic Trigonometry:**
- Sum/Difference formulas; Double/Half angle formulas; solving trigonometric equations.

**Law of Sines & Cosines:**
- Solving non-right triangles; area formulas.

**Vectors:**
- Component form; magnitude; direction; dot product; angle between vectors.

**Parametric Equations:**
- Graphing and eliminating the parameter.

**Polar Coordinates:**
- Converting rectangular to polar; graphing polar curves (limaçons, roses).

**Conic Sections:**
- Analysis of foci, vertices, and directrices.

**Sequences & Series:**
- Sigma notation; infinite geometric series; Binomial Theorem.

**Limits (Intro):**
- Graphic and numeric approach to limits; one-sided limits; continuity.

**Difference Quotient:**
- Understanding the slope of the secant line as $h \to 0$.

---

### Calculus 1 (Differential Calculus)

**Limits & Continuity:**
- Limit laws; infinite limits; vertical asymptotes; Intermediate Value Theorem.

**The Derivative Definition:**
- The limit definition of the derivative; differentiability implying continuity.

**Basic Differentiation Rules:**
- Power, Constant Multiple, Sum/Difference rules; derivatives of $e^x$, $\ln x$, $\sin x$, $\cos x$.

**Product & Quotient Rules:**
- Applied to complex functions.

**Chain Rule:**
- Composite functions; $u$-substitution in differentiation.

**Implicit Differentiation:**
- Finding $dy/dx$ for non-functions; derivatives of inverse trig functions.

**Related Rates:**
- Solving real-world problems with changing variables (ladders, tanks).

**Mean Value Theorem & Rolle's Theorem:**
- Existence of specific tangent slopes.

**Curve Sketching:**
- First and Second Derivative tests; critical points; inflection points; concavity.

**Optimization:**
- Maximizing/minimizing area, volume, cost, etc.

**Antiderivatives:**
- Indefinite integrals; basic integration rules.

**Riemann Sums:**
- Approximating area under a curve using rectangles.

---

### Calculus 2 (Integral Calculus)

**The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:**
- Connecting differentiation and integration; Mean Value Theorem for Integrals.

**Integration by Substitution:**
- $u$-substitution for definite and indefinite integrals.

**Integration by Parts:**
- $\int u \, dv = uv - \int v \, du$.

**Trigonometric Integrals & Substitution:**
- Integrating powers of sine/cosine; using triangle substitution.

**Partial Fractions:**
- Integrating rational functions by decomposition.

**Improper Integrals:**
- Integrals with infinite bounds or discontinuities.

**Area Between Curves:**
- Integrating with respect to $x$ and $y$.

**Volumes of Revolution:**
- Disk and Washer methods; Shell method.

**Arc Length & Surface Area:**
- Integrals for length of curves and surface area of revolution.

**Differential Equations:**
- Separable equations; exponential growth/decay; slope fields.

**Sequences & Series Convergence:**
- Integral, Comparison, Ratio, Root, and Alternating Series tests.

**Power Series:**
- Radius and interval of convergence.

**Taylor & Maclaurin Series:**
- Representing functions as infinite series; Taylor polynomials.

---

### Calculus 3 (Multivariable Calculus)

**Vectors in 3D Space:**
- 3D coordinates; dot product; cross product; equations of lines and planes.

**Vector-Valued Functions:**
- Calculus of vector functions; space curves; projectile motion in 3D.

**Arc Length & Curvature:**
- Unit tangent, normal, and binormal vectors (TNB frame).

**Surfaces:**
- Cylinders and quadric surfaces (ellipsoids, hyperboloids).

**Partial Derivatives:**
- Limits and continuity in multivariable; first and second partials.

**Tangent Planes & Linear Approximation:**
- Local linearization of surfaces.

**The Chain Rule (Multivariable):**
- Tree diagrams for dependent variables.

**Directional Derivatives & Gradient:**
- The gradient vector $\nabla f$; steepest ascent/descent.

**Extrema of Two Variables:**
- Second Partials Test; absolute max/min on closed regions.

**Lagrange Multipliers:**
- Constrained optimization.

**Double Integrals:**
- Iterated integrals; double integrals over general regions.

**Triple Integrals:**
- Integration in 3D space.

**Change of Variables:**
- Double/Triple integrals in Polar, Cylindrical, and Spherical coordinates; The Jacobian.

**Vector Fields:**
- Conservative fields; Curl and Divergence.

**Vector Calculus Theorems:**
- Line Integrals; Green's Theorem; Surface Integrals; Stokes' Theorem; Divergence Theorem.
